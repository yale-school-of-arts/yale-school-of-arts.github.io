The process

How to apply, step by step

  1. 1

    Choose your program

    Pick one of the four MFA study areas that best fits your practice.

  2. 2

    Build your portfolio

    Assemble 16–20 of your strongest recent works to the program’s spec.

  3. 3

    Write your statement

    Tell us about your work, your questions and why now.

  4. 4

    Gather references

    Request three recommendations from people who know your work.

  5. 5

    Submit by the deadline

    Complete the online application and pay the $100 fee by January 7.

  6. 6

    Interview (if invited)

    Shortlisted applicants in some programs are invited to a review.

Questions

Admissions FAQ

The questions prospective students ask us most.

The school offers the Master of Fine Arts (MFA), a two-year, full-time degree in one of four areas: Graphic Design, Painting/Printmaking, Photography, or Sculpture.

Applications for the next MFA cohort are due January 7, 2027. The online portal opens the preceding September. We recommend starting your portfolio early.

Admission is highly selective, with an acceptance rate of about 6%. Decisions are based primarily on the portfolio, alongside the statement and references.

Annual tuition is $48,200 for the prototype’s reference year. Every admitted student is automatically considered for need-based financial aid; no separate scholarship application is required.

You submit 16–20 images (or time-based work where appropriate) representing your strongest recent work. Requirements vary slightly by program — see the Portfolio Requirements page for specifics.

No specific major is required. We welcome applicants from a range of backgrounds; the portfolio and your potential for graduate-level studio work matter most.

You apply to a single area of study per cycle. Choose the program that best fits your practice; faculty in that area review your portfolio.

Shortlisted applicants in some programs are invited to an interview or portfolio review, which may take place on campus or online. This is part of the second round, not the initial application.

Ready when you are

Applications close January 7, 2027. Start early — a strong portfolio takes time.
